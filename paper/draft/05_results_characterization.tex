% §5 Studies I--III: characterization and prediction

This section addresses our research question (\S\ref{sec:intro}) on ARC-Challenge test ($n{=}1{,}172$).
We proceed in three stages: \textbf{characterize} each pre-hoc signal family (Studies I--II), then \textbf{predict} routing opportunity from their combination (Study III).
\textbf{Decision} utility (Study IV) is deferred to \S\ref{sec:routing} until predictive information is established.
All test metrics are evaluation-only; calibration data support $c(q)$ selection and nested model fitting (\S\ref{sec:setup:nested}).
Weak or null association is a valid scientific finding \citep{plaut2024chat}.

\subsection{Study III: Complementary predictive information (RH3)}
\label{sec:char:study3}

\textbf{Question.} Do the model-independent and model-dependent signal families provide \textbf{complementary predictive information} about routing opportunity?

This study evaluates \textbf{prediction} quality---not routing deployment.
\textbf{Primary endpoint:} $\Delta$AUROC from $c(q)$ alone to joint features $c(q){+}H_w{+}m_w$ on the test set, with DeLong test and bootstrap CI.
Positive $\Delta$AUROC indicates that the model-dependent family adds predictive information beyond the model-independent family; near-zero $\Delta$ suggests one family suffices.
