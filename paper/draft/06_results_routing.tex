% §6 Study IV: decision utility (RH4)

Study IV evaluates \textbf{decision} utility: given Study III's assessment of complementary predictive information, can pre-hoc signals support lightweight routing that improves cost--quality over static baselines?
This is distinct from learned pre-hoc estimators \citep{cao2026scope}: we use a simple calibration-fit logistic policy over characterized signals only.
We do not use oracle labels at deployment time.
Median-threshold heuristics are internal sanity checks and are not reported.

\subsection{Held-out policy}
\label{sec:routing:policy}

Study IV is independent of Study III (\S\ref{sec:char:study3}).
We fit on calibration data:
\begin{equation}
\hat{p}(q) = P\bigl(y^{\text{opp}}=1 \mid c(q), H_w(q), m_w(q)\bigr),
\end{equation}
tune $\tau$ by maximizing $U = \text{accuracy} - \lambda \cdot \text{avg.\ cost}$ (\S\ref{sec:setup:utility}), freeze $(\text{weights}, \tau)$, and evaluate on test only.
\textbf{Routing rule:} assign $q$ to $M_s$ if $\hat{p}(q) \geq \tau$; else $M_w$.

\subsection{Baselines and reporting}
\label{sec:routing:baselines}

Following \citet{hu2024routerbench}, we compare static policies and an offline upper bound.
For each $\lambda \in \{0,\,0.05,\,0.10\}$, we report accuracy, average cost, utility $U$, and opportunity recall.
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Always-weak} / \textbf{Always-strong} --- fixed assignment baselines;
  \item \textbf{Learned router} --- calibration-fit logistic + $\tau(\lambda)$;
  \item \textbf{Oracle} --- cheapest successful pool member (offline upper bound).
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Cost model:} weak $=1$, strong $=3$ (\S\ref{sec:setup:metrics}).
